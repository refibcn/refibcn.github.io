Our approach

There's a gap at the heart of most regenerative work: between the ambition and the infrastructure needed to realize it. Projects that should reinforce each other operate in isolation. Ecological and social goals run on separate tracks. Financial systems designed for a different era can't sustain transitions that take decades. We work in that gap — the connective tissue between initiatives, the conditions beneath the visible work.

What we do

We work at the substrate level — building the coordination, shared knowledge, and financial architecture that allow networks of initiatives to act coherently across their territory.

Network coordination

Build the trust and rhythms that let networks move together

Regenerative transitions are, above all, coordination problems. We build the relational and organizational infrastructure that makes sustained collaboration possible.

  • Program facilitation & network accompaniment

    We facilitate multi-stakeholder processes, governance design, and network convenings — the shared rhythms that keep decisions moving, newcomers oriented, and friction from turning into fracture when too many relationships sit on one group.

  • Process alignment & automation

    We map and align workflows across a network — funding pipelines, application tracking, governance handoffs — and automate only where it actually cuts coordination tax instead of adding another layer.

Collective intelligence

Make the territory legible so actors can see the whole

Networks can't align around what they can't see. Through research, mapping, and visualization we build the shared understanding that lets actors recognize themselves as part of something larger.

  • Complex research & visualizations

    Place-based research, social systems mapping, and visualization that make a territory legible — living outputs partners can revise together, not a report that goes stale the moment it's delivered.

  • Collective knowledge infrastructure

    Linked knowledge bases, dashboards, and repositories the whole network can build on — designed for collective editing and complex decisions, not one drive or one person's memory.

Regenerative finance

Finance transitions at the pace and scale regeneration needs

The financial systems that exist weren't built to sustain territorial regeneration. We design and operate alternatives that reward systemic outcomes, not isolated outputs.

  • Grant programs & collaborative funding

    We design and run grant rounds and collaborative treasuries — from local pools to multi-sig funds — so more capital reaches more projects, with governance anyone in the network can verify.

  • Landscape-scale finance strategy

    Portfolio development, fundraising support, and experiments in network-level financial infrastructure — for when you're ready to move beyond one-off grants toward capital that can follow regeneration over time.

Working across a territory or network? We help initiatives move from isolated projects to shared infrastructure — coordination, sensemaking, and finance designed for the scale of regeneration.

Territorial grantmaking locals can trust — and verify

Luiz Fernando Gomez Segala Giulio Quarta

Regenerant Catalunya is a territorial funding program we designed and operated for Gitcoin Grants Round 24 — pooling more than €27K from Catalan donors and global matchers, then distributing it to ten-plus regenerative projects through a cooperative multi-sig treasury. We held the connective layer: partner onboarding, bilingual communications, application flows, evaluation criteria, and the weekly rhythms that kept funders, stewards, and grantees aligned across the round. Phase 2 extends that infrastructure with clearer reporting and longer-term accompaniment. Collective intelligence Network coordination

A portfolio view the whole network can orient around

Andrea Farias

Bioregional Weaving Labs brings together place-based labs across continents; the network needed a shared view of who was doing what, where, and with what resources. We structured their Notion portfolio — relationships, programs, geographies, funding status, and maturity signals — then built portfolio views and maps that let stewards compare initiatives without collapsing local context. Workshops with lab leads shaped the taxonomy so categories reflected how practitioners actually describe their work. The result sits at the intersection of sensemaking and coordination: a legible network surface labs can use to learn from each other, spot gaps, and weave collaborations that respect bioregional difference.

Collective intelligence

Making a bioregion legible for collective decisions

Andrea Farias Luiz Fernando Gomez Segala

The Bioregional Intelligence System is our research and visualization surface for territorial sensemaking — interactive maps and dashboards that show how initiatives, institutions, and landscapes relate across a bioregion such as the Fluvià watershed. Built for complex, collective decision-making, it turns qualitative fieldwork, interviews, and structured datasets into living views rather than static PDFs. Stakeholders can explore actors, capital flows, and gaps; project teams can update sources as conditions shift. We share components with sibling surfaces so sensemaking stays consistent across the ReFi BCN ecosystem while remaining editable by the people who know the territory. Findings can feed public maps and the Bioregional Garden when partners are ready to publish.

Collective intelligence Network coordination

Stewarded knowledge for a high-steppe landscape

Andrea Farias

Altiplano Estepario is a stewarded knowledge commons for one of Europe's largest semi-arid landscapes — documenting regeneration practice, local actors, water stewardship, and learning across the Iberian southeast. We designed and maintain the site as a durable reference for researchers, funders, and practitioners working in dryland social-ecological systems where climate pressure and rural depopulation intersect. Pages link cases, maps, and bibliographies so visitors can follow threads across scale. The project demonstrates how collective intelligence can stay rooted in territory: curated narratives and contribution paths for people who know the land — not a generic platform imposed from outside.

Who we work with

Organizations, networks, and institutions that see ecological and social health as inseparable — and understand that their work needs to operate systemically to affect real change.

  • Cooperatives & solidarity economies

    You build through cooperative and solidarity structures — and need connective infrastructure that fits democratic ownership, not tools designed for extractive scale.

  • Bioeconomy & regenerative agriculture

    Your work crosses landscapes and supply chains, and you need coordination that links rural stewards and urban food actors without flattening either context.

  • Nature restoration initiatives

    Your restoration only holds when the communities on the land shape governance and livelihoods — we work with initiatives that treat ecology and social fabric as one problem.

  • Local & regional governments

    You steward public institutions watching a regenerative transition take shape — and want to align programs and partners around it without smothering what's already emerging.

  • Institutions at territory scale

    You work at watershed or bioregional scale, where place, social fabric, and ecological health have to be designed together — not handed off to separate silos.

  • Global networks & researchers

    You're part of a federation or research community testing regenerative finance and governance — and need a local node that can bridge cooperative practice on the ground with network-scale infrastructure.
